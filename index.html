Accumulative Death of COVID-19 from Jan 3 2020 to Sep 20 2023

The visualization displayed on the left provides insight into the cumulative death toll since the COVID-19 outbreak up to the year 2023. It's evident that certain countries, including Russia, the United States of America, Brazil, and others, have witnessed the most substantial cumulative death compared to other nations. The severity of the accumulated death data depicted on the map underscores the current peril posed by COVID-19.

Crude Mortality Rate

The following graph unequivocally illustrates that the Oceania continent claims the highest COVID-19 mortality rate. A plausible inference can be drawn, suggesting that the level of medical infrastructure in Oceania may not be as advanced as in other continents, thereby contributing to the elevated crude mortality rate in this region.

Infection Fatality Rate

From the presented graph, it is evident that in the early months of 2020, each continent experienced a pronounced surge in infection fatality rates. As time progressed toward the year 2023, we observe a gradual decline in the line graph. This decline could lead us to surmise that during the year 2020, the seriousness of the COVID-19 virus was not fully appreciated, and no vaccine was available, contributing to the high infection fatality rates. In the subsequent years following 2020, the fatality rates became less severe. However, in 2022, there is a gradual resurgence in the infection fatality rate. This resurgence suggests that, as of 2022, there may still be no definitive cure for COVID-19.
